About STRXplorer

A Bioengineering Capstone Project

Project Overview

STRXplorer is an interactive web platform designed to visualize and explore associations between Short Tandem Repeats (STRs) and complex human traits. This tool bridges the gap between complex genomic data and accessible scientific exploration, making STR research more approachable for researchers worldwide.

Short Tandem Repeats represent approximately 3% of the human genome yet have been largely overlooked in traditional genome-wide association studies (GWAS). Our platform integrates STR locus plots with Manhattan plots, enabling researchers to seamlessly explore both population-level associations and individual STR effects — a capability that has never been available before in a unified interface.

This work is intended to be integrated into WebSTR, a comprehensive population-wide database of short tandem repeat variation in humans, expanding its analytical capabilities and making STR research more accessible to the broader scientific community.

Educational Context

This project was developed as part of UC San Diego's Bioengineering Undergraduate Capstone Program, a year-long sequence designed to provide senior-level students with comprehensive design experience.

Capstone Design Course Sequence (BENG 187A-D)

A multiquarter upper-division sequence totaling ten quarter-units, including:

  • BENG 187A: Project selection and professional considerations
  • BENG 187B: Design methodology and planning
  • BENG 187C: Implementation and development
  • BENG 187D: Presentation and professional communication

The capstone program encompasses various bioengineering domains including biomechanics, systems bioengineering, nanoscale and molecular bioengineering, organ system bioengineering, tissue engineering and regenerative medicine, and bioinstrumentation.

As students in the bioengineering department under the bioinformatics track, we chose to develop a computational project that would build valuable skills for our future careers as bioinformaticians while contributing meaningful tools to the genomics research community.

Scientific Foundation

Foundational Research

This platform is inspired by groundbreaking research on STR contributions to complex traits:

Margoliash, J., Fuchs, S., Li, Y., Zhang, X., Massarat, A., Goren, A., & Gymrek, M. (2023). Polymorphic short tandem repeats make widespread contributions to blood and serum traits. Cell Genomics, 3(12), 100458.
Lundström, O., Verbiest, M.A., Xia, F., Jam, H.Z., Zlobec, I., Anisimova, M., & Gymrek, M. (2023). WebSTR: A Population-wide Database of Short Tandem Repeat Variation in Humans. Journal of Molecular Biology, 435(22), 168260.

These studies demonstrate that STRs account for 5.2%–7.6% of causal variants identifiable from GWASs for blood traits and reveal novel therapeutic targets in the previously understudied 3% of the genome.

Integration with WebSTR Ecosystem

Our STRXplorer is designed as a key component of the broader WebSTR ecosystem — a comprehensive database aggregating STR data from multiple studies spanning over 1.7 million genomic loci across diverse human populations.

WebSTR currently provides:

  • Population-specific allele frequencies
  • Mutation rates and imputation metrics
  • Associations with gene expression levels
  • Programmatic API access for researchers

Our visualization platform adds crucial GWAS analysis capabilities, making complex trait associations accessible through interactive Manhattan and STR locus plots.
